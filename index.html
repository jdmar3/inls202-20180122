Today I am going to talk to you about looking at information systems through a critical lens, not just for their utility, but also for understanding the social and historical context of their creation and use.

Why epistemology?

And first: what is epistemology?

Worldview as projection

Our worldview is a projection of our epistemology onto the world around us. It is the filter through which we read the world. It is the framework within which we construct and interpret what we experience.

Information interactions

We are concerned in this field with how people interact with information: how they find and retrieve it and how they use it. As part of information use, we're interested in how people create, share, disseminate, and divulge information.

Skeuomorphism

skeu·o·morph

(/ˈskjuːəˌmɔːrf,) noun

an object or feature that imitates the design of a similar artifact made from another material.

"the pottery box with a square lid is a skeuomorph of a twilled basketry container"

skeu·o·morph

(/ˈskjuːəˌmɔːrf,) noun

COMPUTING: an element of a graphical user interface that mimics a physical object.

Another poll

So all of those are different, obviously, but in each case, when you use them, you are typically broadcasting a variety of different personal information. There are mechanisms in place to help you manage that activity. So, let's see how aware we are of those.

Privacy settings?

How many of you have recently checked the privacy settings on your Facebook accounts? Or maybe read the documentation that comes along with a change in the privacy policies?

Permissions?

How about permissions? Do you keep your Instagram, Facebook, or Twitter accounts set to private so that only people you follow can see your posts?

EULA/TOU/TOS?

How many of you have read, in full, an End User License Agreement, or Terms of Service/Terms of Use in order to see how your information is being handled?

API?

API stands for "Application Programming Interface." How many people here have read the documentation for one of these to see what kind of information can be automatically extracted from a platform for the purposes of data mining? No one? These are in use at all times on most social media platforms. A lot of platforms also encourage researchers to use them to sample for research purposes.

Is someone watching you?

How many of you think that there is someone watching you online? If you answered "no," I'm sorry to have to disappoint you.
